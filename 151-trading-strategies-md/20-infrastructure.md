Infrastructure
Broadly, investing in infrastructure includes investing in long-term projects
such as transportation (roads, bridges, tunnels, railways, ports, airports, etc.),
telecommunications (transmission cables, satellites, towers, etc.), utilities
(electricity generation, gas or electricity transmission or distribution, water
supply, sewage, waste, etc.), energy (including but not limited to renewable
energy), healthcare (hospitals, clinics, senior homes, etc.), educational facilities
(schools, universities, research institutes, etc.), etc. An investor can gain expo-
sure to infrastructure assets through different direct or indirect investments
such private equity-type investments (e.g., via unlisted infrastructure funds),
listed infrastructure funds, stocks of publicly traded infrastructure companies,
municipal bonds earmarked to infrastructure projects, etc.

investments. One investment strategy is to use infrastructure assets to improve
risk-adjusted returns of well-diversiﬁed portfolios, e.g., via tracking ETFs,
